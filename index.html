Ben's Personal Finance Tips

With the amount of ways to save and earn cash back these days, I found it very confusing at first to determine which apps were legit and which apps were scams. After much exploring I found several that are very reliable.

Full disclosure, the links to the apps below are referral links. If this summary helps you find new ways to earn and save, please consider going through the links when you're signing up.

Robinhood is a stock trading app that gained popularity by eliminating broker fees. This link gives you a free stock valued between $3 and $150 when you sign up.

Coinbase is a app that allows you to easily purchase cryptocurrencies on your mobile device. This link gives you $10 in bitcoin when you spend $100.

Ally is an online bank with one of the best high-yield savings accounts on the market. It is currently at 1.90% APY (1.9% interest each year) as of August 2019.

Rakuten (formerly Ebates) is a platform to give you additional cash back on your purchases simply by going thru their portal. Most purchases will save you around 5% but I have gotten as much as 40% cash back.

"What news platforms do you use for personal finance?"


Stocktwits gives a humorous and informative recap of the stock (and crypto) market each day. Sign up here for the daily email.

For breakdowns on how to maximize your points on credit cards (especially through travel) The Points Guy has very detailed explanations.

For weekly online shopping deals, bank account bonuses, and updates to credit card perks, Doctor of Credit is usually the first to know.
